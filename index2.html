Welcome

My academic journey began with a B.Sc. in Chemistry, Botany, and Microbiology from Matsyodari Shikshan Sanstha’s Arts, Commerce and Science College, Ambad, Maharashtra (2011–2014), followed by an M.Sc. in Organic Chemistry from Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University, Aurangabad (2014–2016). These formative years solidified my passion for chemistry and laid the groundwork for advanced research. Driven by a deep interest in supramolecular chemistry, I pursued a Ph.D. at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala (2017–2023), under the mentorship of Prof. Kana M. Sureshan. I then moved to the Washington University in St. Louis, USA, for my first postdoctoral research with Prof. Chenfeng Ke. Currently, I am pursuing my second postdoctoral research at Ghent University, Belgium, under the supervision of Prof. Kristof Van Hecke in the XStruct lab.

Research Interests

I’m a researcher in supramolecular and polymer chemistry, with a keen interest in developing advanced materials aimed at promoting a sustainable future. My work lies at the intersection of materials science and organic synthesis, where I tackle complex challenges in molecular design and functional material development. My research journey began with a focus on supramolecular chemistry, where I synthesized crystalline polyamides through topochemical azide–alkyne cycloaddition polymerization conducted under solvent- and catalyst-free conditions. This approach allowed for the precise construction of polymeric architectures within crystal lattices. I also explored the thermoresponsive behavior of organic crystals during topochemical transformations, observing remarkable effects such as cracking, expansion, and self-healing—characteristics that point toward the development of smart, responsive materials. Currently, my research extends into the design and synthesis of porous organic frameworks and molecular cages, which hold significant potential for applications including gas storage, molecular separation, and catalysis. More broadly, I’m fascinated by the interplay between solid-state reactivity and molecular organization, and I aim to contribute to the rational design of next-generation functional materials through innovative chemistry.
